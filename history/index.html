Parish information

History

The story of St Mary's is held in stone, memory, and parish records, and still continues in everyday worship.

Historical overview

St Mary the Virgin has served Henbury for many centuries. The building has changed over time, but worship, prayer, and parish life have remained at its heart.

  1. 12th-13th centuries

    Norman origins and medieval growth

    The earliest stonework points to a Norman church on this site. By the later medieval period, the church had become a central place for worship and village life.

  2. 15th century

    Tower and churchyard expansion

    Much of the tower and parts of the nave were rebuilt or strengthened as the parish expanded, leaving the strong Perpendicular character seen today.

  3. 19th century

    Victorian restoration

    As with many parish churches, substantial restoration work was carried out to repair the fabric and adapt seating and liturgical space.

  4. 20th century

    War memory and parish continuity

    The church became a place of remembrance for local people lost in conflict. Parish records from this period also show steady everyday ministry through social change.

  5. Today

    Living parish, active churchyard

    St Mary's continues as a worshipping community and a historic landmark for Henbury, with regular services, pastoral ministry, and shared local events.

Churchyard

Churchyard & memorials

  • The churchyard includes memorials and graves from different periods, each reflecting the social history of Henbury.
  • Interpretation of memorials linked to slavery and enslavement is handled with care, factual language, and historical context.
  • The churchyard also includes the grave of Scipio Africanus, often described as an LGBT landmark grave as well as an important site in Black British history.

Historical notes on this page are intended as brief introductions. We welcome corrections and additions from local historians and families with parish links.
